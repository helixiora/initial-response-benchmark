The Renaissance: Rebirth of Classical Learning

The Renaissance was a period of cultural, artistic, political, and economic "rebirth" that spanned roughly from the 14th to the 17th century, beginning in Italy and later spreading throughout Europe. The term "Renaissance" means "rebirth" in French and refers to the revival of interest in classical antiquity, learning, and values.

Key Features:

1. Humanism:
   - Focus on human potential and achievements
   - Study of classical texts from ancient Greece and Rome
   - Emphasis on education, rhetoric, and moral philosophy
   - Notable humanists: Petrarch, Erasmus, Thomas More

2. Art and Architecture:
   - Realistic portrayal of the human form
   - Use of perspective and anatomical accuracy
   - Patronage by wealthy families and the Church
   - Masters: Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, Raphael, Botticelli, Donatello

3. Scientific Revolution:
   - Observation-based approach to understanding nature
   - Challenging of ancient authorities like Aristotle and Galen
   - Key figures: Copernicus, Galileo, Vesalius

4. Political Developments:
   - Rise of powerful city-states in Italy
   - Machiavelli's "The Prince" and political realism
   - Strengthening of centralized monarchies in Northern Europe

5. Printing Revolution:
   - Gutenberg's printing press (c. 1440)
   - Wider dissemination of knowledge
   - Increased literacy rates

The Renaissance laid the groundwork for the modern era by promoting individualism, critical thinking, scientific inquiry, and artistic expression.
